Structure 27



A midden from Structure 27 was dated to the Buk phase of the Postclassic. Some species identified in the midden include barracuda, grouper, jackfish, parrotfish, river catfish, snapper, iguana, river and pond turtle, crocodile, broket and white-tailed deer, and shark (Williams et al., 2009).



Researchers were able to use the faunal remains to help understand human diet through isotopic analysis.



Williams and colleagues (2009) have explained that, due to their proximity, Marco Gonzalez and San Pedro probably had similar food access.



Additionally, Structures 26, 27, and 28 are made up of thousands of conch shells. Some of the species of conch identified include Queen Conch, West Indian chank, and helmet shells (Simmons and Graham, 2017). In regards to Structure 27, there would have been special equipment and boats that would have been needed to collect species such as jewfish (Emery and Graham, 2003).
